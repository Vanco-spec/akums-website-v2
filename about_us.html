WHO WE ARE

The Association of Kenyatta University Medical Students (AKUMS) is the official body representing all medical students within the School of Medicine and allied health sciences at Kenyatta University. Since its founding in 2008, AKUMS has been a pillar of student leadership, academic growth, and community service.

As a member of the Medical Students Association of Kenya (MSAKE) — which itself is part of the International Federation of Medical Students’ Associations (IFMSA) — AKUMS connects its members to a global network of medical students advocating for health equity, education, and leadership.

Through mentorship, research, advocacy, and humanitarian projects, AKUMS empowers students to become ethical, skilled, and compassionate healthcare professionals who contribute meaningfully to society.

Beyond the classroom, AKUMS fosters a strong sense of community through health outreach programs, sporting events, and cultural activities that strengthen collaboration among students. These initiatives not only nurture holistic growth but also cultivate the spirit of service and unity that defines the future healthcare professionals of Kenyatta University.

OUR COMMUNITY & ACADEMIC TRADITIONS

At AKUMS, we don’t just study medicine — we live it through service, celebration, and professional growth. From life-saving blood drives and spirited tournaments to the symbolic lab coat and stethoscope ceremonies, our initiatives bring purpose, unity, and pride to every member.

Blood Drives

In collaboration with the Kenya National Blood Transfusion Service (KNBTS) and partner hospitals, AKUMS holds regular campus blood drives that save hundreds of lives each year.

AKUMS Tournament

A highlight of our social calendar — the annual AKUMS Tournament unites students across faculties for friendly competition in football, volleyball, basketball, and athletics.

Lab Coat Ceremony

A proud milestone for our new medical students, the Lab Coat Ceremony marks their official entry into the medical profession — a moment of reflection, pride, and commitment to ethical practice.

Stethoscope Ceremony

The Stethoscope Ceremony honors our clinical students as they transition from theory to patient care. It celebrates empathy, professionalism, and the trust placed in future doctors.

WHAT WE DO

AKUMS offers a broad spectrum of programs and initiatives to help medical students grow academically, professionally, and socially.

Academic Mentorship

We support students via tutorials, mentorship sessions, peer-led workshops, and study groups to nurture excellence.

Community Health Outreach

We conduct medical camps, health education drives, screenings, and awareness campaigns in under-served communities.

Leadership & Innovation

We build leadership capabilities via seminars, innovation challenges, teamwork projects, and idea incubators.

Global Partnerships

We collaborate with international and local health bodies, giving students exposure to global health systems and opportunities.

Student Welfare

We promote well-being via advocacy, support services, welfare initiatives, and community among fellow medical students.
